Complete as Frases
✏️
📝 Preencha os espaços e clique em "Verificar"!
1. The whole stadium was cheering loudly. Everyone was the home team.
Use o verbo que significa 'torcer por' no gerúndio (-ing). *ROOT FOR*
2. Nobody believed the could beat the champions, but they won 3-0!
Palavra que significa o 'azarão' — o time menos favorito a vencer. *UNDERDOG*
3. After scoring the winning goal, the players ran straight to the to celebrate with their coach.
Lugar onde os jogadores ficam antes e depois do jogo. *LOCKER ROOM*
4. The fans were waving their team's in the air to show their support.
A palavra para 'camisas do time' no plural. *JERSEYS*
5. Tourists from all over the world come to Rio de Janeiro just to see in person.
O nome em inglês da famosa estátua do Rio de Janeiro. *CHRIST THE REDEEMER*
6. The captain showed real by organizing a charity event for injured players without anyone asking him.
Palavra que descreve agir por conta própria, proativamente. *INITIATIVE*

Perguntas Abertas
💬
🖊️ Responda em inglês! Clique em "Ver modelo" para se inspirar.
1. Have you ever rooted for an underdog team or athlete? What happened during the game?
Yes! I once rooted for a small local soccer team in the state championship. Nobody believed they could win, but they showed great solidarity and attitude on the field. They scored twice in the second half and almost won. It was incredible to cheer for them!
2. Why do you think prejudice in sports stadiums is a serious problem? What can fans and players do about it?
Prejudice in sports is a serious problem because stadiums should be places of joy and solidarity for everyone. Fans can take initiative by speaking up when they see prejudiced behavior. Athletes can also use their influence to raise awareness — they are role models and people listen to them.
3. If a foreign tourist asked you about Christ the Redeemer, what would you tell them in English?
Christ the Redeemer is one of Brazil's most famous landmarks and one of the Seven Wonders of the Modern World. It is a huge statue located on top of Corcovado Mountain in Rio de Janeiro. It represents peace and welcomes everyone who visits Brazil. You can see the whole city from up there — it's absolutely amazing!
