Complete as Frases
✏️
📝 Preencha os espaços e clique em "Verificar"!
1. you like to try the new menu tonight?
Use o auxiliar modal *WOULD* no início da pergunta para fazer um convite educado.
2. I'd love to go out, but I to study tonight.
Use *HAVE* (have to = ter que) como desculpa após aceitar um convite parcialmente.
3. She tried the food at the new restaurant — and she loved it!
Use *EVEN* em frases afirmativas para expressar surpresa ('até mesmo').
4. I don't know if the restaurant is available tonight.
Use *EVEN* em frases negativas para reforçar a negação ('nem mesmo').
5. After performing on stage, all the musicians just wanted to
Use o phrasal verb *CHILL OUT*, que significa relaxar depois de algo intenso.
6. Honestly, I wouldn't know how to with that kind of situation.
Use o verbo *DEAL* (deal with = lidar com) para falar sobre como enfrentar situações difíceis.

4
Perguntas Abertas
💬
🖊️ Responda em inglês! Clique em "Ver modelo" para se inspirar.
1. Would you like to try a new sport or activity? Why or why not?
Yes, I'd like to try yoga. I've always wanted to be more flexible, but I need to find a class that's available near my home first. Honestly, I don't even know where to sign up!
2. Is there something you don't even know how to start? What is it and why?
Honestly, I don't even know how to cook a proper meal. I'd like to learn, but I need to buy the right utensils — a fork, a knife, a spoon — and watch some tutorials first. I wouldn't even know which recipe to try!
3. What would you do to chill out after a long day? Would you deal with any problems, or would you just relax?
I would put on some old records and sit on the couch. I wouldn't deal with any problems at all — I'd just relax and enjoy the music. I even bought some new albums last week to help me unwind after work!
