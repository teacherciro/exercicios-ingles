Complete as Frases
✏️
📝 Preencha os espaços e clique em "Verificar"!
1. The menu has only two options: pasta or salad. dish do you prefer?
As opções já foram definidas no menu, então use *WHICH* em vez de 'what'.
2. After the long trip, they very tired.
O sujeito THEY usa *WERE* no passado do verbo TO BE.
3. My brother married near a beautiful church last summer.
A expressão 'to get married' no passado usa *GOT* married.
4. house is next to the church. It's very old.
Use o possessivo *THEIR* para indicar 'deles/delas' (plural de they).
5. She breakfast before she ran to the church.
O passado de 'to have' é *HAD*. 'To have breakfast' = tomar café da manhã.
6. He put the letter near the door because nobody was home.
Para negar no passado sem TO BE, use *DIDN'T* + verbo na forma base.

Perguntas Abertas
💬
🖊️ Responda em inglês! Clique em "Ver modelo" para se inspirar.
1. What did you have for breakfast this morning? Was it near your home?
I had eggs and coffee for breakfast this morning. Yes, the café where I went was near my house, next to the church on my street.
2. Which do you prefer: a short holiday or a long vacation? Why?
I prefer a long vacation because I can discover new places and relax more. A holiday is too short — I was always busy and didn't get to walk around or explore.
3. Were you busy last week? What did you discover or learn?
Yes, I was very busy last week. I discovered a new restaurant next to my office and I got to try their lunch. It was great — that's sick!
