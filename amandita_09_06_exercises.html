2
Complete as Frases
✏️
📝 Preencha os espaços e clique em "Verificar"!
1. I travel to Japan next month. (afirmativo com will)
Use o auxiliar *WILL* para fazer uma afirmação sobre o futuro.
2. She going to watch Devil Wears Prada tonight.
Complete com o verbo TO BE correto. Com 'she', usamos *IS*.
3. Are you going to the apartment?
Use o verbo *BUY* no infinitivo. Depois de 'going to', sempre usamos o verbo base.
4. I travel to Japan. I changed my plans! (negação com will)
Use a contração negativa de 'will not': *WON'T*.
5. He going to buy the apartment. It's too expensive. (negativo)
Com 'he', a negação de 'going to' é *ISN'T* (is not).
6. I'm watch Devil Wears Prada this weekend. (forma informal)
Use a forma INFORMAL de 'going to': *GONNA*.

Perguntas Abertas
💬
🖊️ Responda em inglês! Clique em "Ver modelo" para se inspirar.
1. What are you going to do next weekend? Use 'going to' and describe at least two plans.
Next weekend, I am going to visit my family and we are going to watch a movie together. I am also going to cook a special dinner for everyone.
2. Tell me about something you will NOT do this year. Use 'won't' or 'am not going to' and explain why.
I won't travel to another country this year because I am saving money. I am not going to buy a new phone either, because my current one still works fine.
3. Ask your teacher three questions about their future plans using 'Will you...?' or 'Are you going to...?'
Will you travel anywhere this summer? Are you going to watch any new movies this month? Are you going to learn a new language next year?
