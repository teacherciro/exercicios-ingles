Complete as Frases
✏️
📝 Preencha os espaços e clique em "Verificar"!
1. The spa offers several amazing for couples, including massage and hot tub access.
Em inglês, 'pacotes' de serviços ou viagens se diz *PACKAGES*.
2. That to me — are you sure the baby shower is on Saturday?
Para dizer que algo 'soa estranho', use o verbo *SOUNDS* seguido de um adjetivo.
3. When I was a child, my family and I go on hikes every weekend.
Para falar de um hábito do passado que não acontece mais, use *USED TO*.
4. There aren't currencies accepted here — only the dollar.
Use *MANY* com substantivos contáveis no plural, como currencies (moedas).
5. Your cousin your mother — they have the same smile!
Para dizer que alguém 'parece-se com' outra pessoa ou coisa, use *LOOKS LIKE* seguido de um substantivo.
6. All the massage chairs are this week — we should get one!
Quando um produto está em promoção ou com desconto, dizemos que está *ON SALE*.

4
Perguntas Abertas
💬
🖊️ Responda em inglês! Clique em "Ver modelo" para se inspirar.
1. Have you ever been to a spa or used a hot tub? What did it feel like?
Yes, I went to a spa last year with my cousin. It was very relaxing! The hot tub was warm and felt amazing. They also had massage packages on sale, so we got one. That was adventurous of us!
2. What did you use to do on weekends when you were a child? Do you still do any of those things?
When I was a child, I used to hike with my family every Sunday morning. We don't do it much anymore, but sometimes I still hike with friends. I also used to visit my cousin every weekend — we were very close.
3. Look at someone you know well. Use 'look', 'seem', and 'look like' in three different sentences to describe them.
My cousin looks like my grandmother — they have the same brown eyes and smile. Today she seems a little tired because she worked late. But she always looks beautiful, even when she's exhausted!
