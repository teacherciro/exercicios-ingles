1. Describe how to make your favorite dish. Use at least four kitchen verbs from the lesson and sequence words (first, then, next, finally).
My favorite dish is mashed potatoes. First, I chop the potatoes into chunks and boil them in salted water. Then, I drain the water. Next, I add butter and melt it with the potatoes. Finally, I mash everything together until it is smooth and creamy.
2. What is the difference between a seamstress, a dressmaker, and a tailor? Can you use each word in a sentence?
A seamstress is a woman who sews and repairs clothes professionally. For example: 'The seamstress fixed my torn shirt.' A dressmaker specializes in custom dresses and clothes. For example: 'The dressmaker created a beautiful gown for the wedding.' A tailor makes and adjusts clothes, often suits and jackets. For example: 'The tailor adjusted my jacket to fit perfectly.'
3. Look at these words: elbow, elbow macaroni, and silverware. What do they mean? Try to use each one in a sentence.
These words are all interesting! 'Elbow' is a part of the body — the joint in the middle of your arm. For example: 'I hit my elbow on the table.' 'Elbow macaroni' is a type of pasta shaped like a bent elbow. For example: 'I use elbow macaroni to make mac and cheese.' 'Silverware' is the word for forks, knives, and spoons. For example: 'Please put the silverware on the table before dinner.'
